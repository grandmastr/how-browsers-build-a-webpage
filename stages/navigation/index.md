# Navigation

The very first step involved in how browsers build a webpage is Navigating to the webpage. This could be in the form of
entering a URL into the address bar, clicking on a link, or even submitting a form. This triggers a series of events
that we will explore in the next section. Even though the navigation process doesn't take long, the web optimization
goal here is to try to reduce the delays caused by latency and bandwidth.
I think it's important to define the term latency here. Latency is the amount of time it takes to transmit bytes
between the server and the client.

The navigation process involves the following steps:

- [DNS Lookup](./dns-lookup.md)
- [TCP Handshake](./tcp-handshake.md)
- [TLS Negotiation](./tls-negotiation.md)
- [HTTP Request](./http-request.md)
